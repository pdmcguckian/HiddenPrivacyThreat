Modern smart home IoT products provide us with convenience and utility. However, they absolutely destroy our right to privacy. Already, Google is using your smart home data to target adds and one day they may start selling it to third parties like insurers or recruiters.
You might think this isn't a problem, as companies like Google typically encrypt the audio and video data leaving devices, meaning they can't see what you're doing.
This may be true, but they can still record device 'meta data' (e.g. when it's turned on, sending data etc.).When you have enough devices in your house this simple data can be used to infer incredibly sensitive information about you.
To prove this I have simulated smart home metadata collection and am using it to try to predict sensitive information about me. To see how, watch the video.
So far I have been able to predict sleep quality and alcohol consumption to a 61% and 78% accuracy, and I am only a month in. Over the next few months, I will keep adding prediction metrics to this site.
